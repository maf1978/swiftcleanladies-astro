Recurring cleaning is a standing weekly, biweekly, or monthly plan that keeps kitchens, baths, and floors at baseline. One-time cleaning is a single booked visit — often a fixed 2 to 5-hour slot with two professionals — for guests, move help, or a catch-up reset. Choose recurring when you want steady upkeep; choose one-time when you need a defined window without an ongoing schedule.

## What Is One-Time Cleaning?

[One-time cleaning](/services/one-time-cleaning/) is booked for a single date and time block. You rank priority rooms. The pair works that list inside the purchased window. It fits guests, parties, move deadlines, or a trial before recurring service. See [one-time cleaning before guests](/blog/one-time-cleaning-before-guests/).

## Which Should I Choose?

**Choose recurring if:** the home gets dirty on a predictable cycle and you want weekends back.

**Choose one-time if:** you have a deadline (guests, move, event) or want to test the team before a plan.

**Choose deep cleaning first if:** buildup is heavy. A [deep cleaning](/services/deep-cleaning/) resets details that maintenance visits do not fully cover. Related: [deep cleaning vs recurring](/blog/deep-cleaning-vs-recurring-cleaning/).
