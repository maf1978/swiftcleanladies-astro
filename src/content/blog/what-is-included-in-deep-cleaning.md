A deep cleaning includes a detailed reset of kitchens and bathrooms, baseboards and trim, higher dusting, appliance interiors when scoped, and floor care beyond a light maintenance visit. It targets buildup that regular recurring cleaning maintains but does not fully remove. ## Key Takeaways

- Deep cleaning goes past surface wipe-downs into detail zones.
- Common inclusions: baths, kitchens, baseboards, fans/blinds dusting, appliance interiors as agreed.
- Recurring cleaning keeps the baseline; deep cleaning restores it.
- Florida humidity makes grout, vents, and sills higher priority — see the [Florida deep clean checklist](/blog/deep-cleaning-checklist-florida-homes/).

## What Does a Deep Cleaning Usually Include?

**Bathrooms:** toilets, tubs/showers, sinks, mirrors, floors, tracks and detail scrubbing as needed.

**Kitchen:** counters, sink, stovetop, exterior appliances, microwave interior, and oven/fridge interiors when included in scope.

**Throughout:** dusting of reachable fixtures and blinds, full baseboard wipe-down, vacuuming and mopping, cobwebs, high-touch switches and handles.

**Often added or scoped separately:** interior windows, heavy grout restoration, inside cabinets, organization projects.

For maintenance-only scope, see [what house cleaning includes](/blog/what-does-house-cleaning-include/).

## What Is Not Always Included?

Deep cleaning is not mold remediation, exterior window washing, or handyman repairs. Locked or inaccessible rooms are skipped. Cluttered floors limit vacuuming until items are cleared — use the [prep checklist](/blog/what-to-do-before-professional-cleaning-visit/).

## Deep Cleaning vs Recurring Cleaning

Recurring visits keep kitchens, baths, and floors usable week to week. Deep cleaning resets sticky film, appliance interiors, and baseboards so recurring stays faster. Full comparison: [deep cleaning vs recurring cleaning](/blog/deep-cleaning-vs-recurring-cleaning/).
