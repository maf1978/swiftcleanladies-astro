- A standard recurring clean covers kitchens, bathrooms, living areas, bedrooms, and floors on a rotating schedule (weekly, biweekly, or monthly).
- Laundry, dishes, and pet waste are typically not included unless you arrange them as add-ons.

## What Tasks Are Included in a Standard House Cleaning Service

A standard house cleaning service covers surface dusting, kitchen and bathroom sanitizing, floor care, and general tidying of living spaces. These are the tasks that keep your home functional and presentable week to week without tackling heavy buildup.

Here is what a typical recurring cleaning checklist looks like:

**Kitchen**

- Wipe down countertops and backsplash
- Clean stovetop and exterior of appliances
- Wipe sink and faucet
- Clean microwave interior
- Spot-clean cabinet fronts
- Empty trash and replace liner

**Bathrooms**

- Scrub and sanitize toilets, tubs, and showers
- Wipe down sinks and countertops
- Clean mirrors
- Restock toilet paper (if provided)
- Empty trash and replace liner

**Living Areas and Bedrooms**

- Dust furniture, shelves, and decor
- Wipe down flat surfaces
- Vacuum carpets and rugs
- Mop hard floors
- Make beds (if requested)
- Straighten pillows and blankets

**Throughout the Home**

- Vacuum and mop all accessible floors
- Dust baseboards at eye level (not full baseboard scrub)
- Wipe light switches and door handles
- Remove cobwebs from reachable corners

## Is Vacuuming and Mopping Included in House Cleaning

Yes, vacuuming and mopping are standard inclusions in nearly every recurring house cleaning visit. Floor care is one of the core tasks that separates a professional cleaning from a quick tidy-up.

Cleaners typically vacuum all carpeted areas and rugs first, then mop hard-surface floors (tile, hardwood, laminate, vinyl) with appropriate cleaners for each material. In homes with pets, this usually includes pet hair removal from floors and visible furniture surfaces.

One common edge case: if rooms are cluttered with items on the floor, cleaners may vacuum around obstacles rather than moving large piles of laundry, toys, or boxes. Clear floors mean more thorough vacuuming and mopping.

## Does House Cleaning Include Laundry and Dishes

No, laundry and dishes are not standard inclusions in a recurring cleaning service. Most cleaning companies, including SwiftClean Ladies, focus on surfaces, floors, and sanitizing rather than personal chores like washing clothes or running the dishwasher.

That said, some services allow you to add these tasks as custom requests. If you need dishes loaded or laundry started, mention it during your walkthrough so the team can factor the time into your visit.

**What is usually not included without special arrangement:**

- Washing, folding, or putting away laundry
- Washing dishes by hand or loading/unloading dishwasher
- Ironing
- Pet feeding or waste cleanup
- Meal preparation

If your priority is getting the kitchen counters and sink clean, loading the dishwasher yourself before the team arrives lets them focus on scrubbing surfaces instead of dealing with dishes.

## How Often Should You Get a Professional House Cleaning

Most homes benefit from biweekly recurring cleaning, which keeps dust, grime, and bathroom buildup manageable without becoming overwhelming. Weekly service works well for busy households, large families, or homes with pets. Monthly service suits smaller homes or households that maintain things well between visits.

Here is a simple guide for choosing frequency:

| Household Type | Recommended Frequency | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Single person, no pets | Monthly or biweekly | Lower traffic means slower buildup |
| Couple, no pets | Biweekly | Standard upkeep for moderate use |
| Family with kids | Biweekly or weekly | Kitchens and baths get heavy daily use |
| Pet owners | Weekly or biweekly | Pet hair, dander, and muddy paws add up fast |
| Busy professionals | Biweekly | Keeps the home at baseline without weekly commitment |
| Large home (4+ bedrooms) | Weekly or biweekly | More surface area means more dust and dirt |

Choose weekly if your kitchen and bathrooms need constant attention. Choose biweekly if you can keep up with light daily tidying. Choose monthly if your home stays relatively clean between visits but you want a professional reset.

## What's the Difference Between Regular Cleaning and Deep Cleaning

Regular cleaning maintains your home at a baseline level, while deep cleaning tackles accumulated buildup, overlooked areas, and tasks that need more time and effort. A deep clean is usually recommended before starting a recurring schedule, or every 3 to 6 months as a reset.

**Regular (recurring) cleaning covers:**

- Surface dusting and wiping
- Kitchen and bathroom sanitizing
- Floor vacuuming and mopping
- General tidying

**Deep cleaning adds:**

- Baseboards (full scrub, top to bottom)
- Inside oven and refrigerator
- Cabinet fronts (detailed wipe-down)
- Light fixtures and ceiling fans
- Window sills and tracks
- Behind and under appliances (where accessible)
- Detailed tile and grout scrubbing
- Door frames and switch plates

For a detailed comparison, read our guide on [deep cleaning vs recurring cleaning](/blog/deep-cleaning-vs-recurring-cleaning/).

## Do Professional Cleaners Clean Windows and Baseboards

Windows and baseboards are partially included in recurring cleaning, but the scope is limited. Here is how it typically breaks down:

**Recurring cleaning includes:**

- Dusting baseboards at eye level (quick wipe)
- Wiping window sills
- Cleaning interior glass only if specifically requested

**Deep cleaning includes:**

- Full baseboard scrub (top to bottom, including corners)
- Window sills and tracks detailed
- Interior window glass
- Blinds and shutters wiped down

Exterior window cleaning is almost never included in standard house cleaning. That requires specialized equipment and is usually handled by a separate window-washing service.

## What Shouldn't You Expect a House Cleaner to Do

House cleaners focus on cleaning surfaces, floors, and fixtures. There are several tasks that fall outside the standard scope for safety, liability, or practical reasons.

**Typically not included:**

- Exterior window cleaning (requires special equipment)
- Gutter cleaning
- Mold remediation or biohazard cleanup
- Pest control
- Heavy furniture moving (items must be movable by one person)
- Lawn care or exterior maintenance
- Pet waste cleanup or litter box scooping
- Laundry, ironing, or dishwashing (unless arranged)
- Repairs or handyman tasks

**What rooms do house cleaners usually skip?**

Rooms that are locked, under active renovation, or explicitly excluded in your cleaning plan will be skipped. If a room is cluttered to the point where surfaces are not accessible, cleaners will tidy what they can but may not fully clean that space.

A common mistake is expecting cleaners to organize clutter. Decluttering is a separate task. If you need help sorting closets, pantries, or storage spaces, our [home organization service](/services/home-organization/) is designed for that specific need.

## Should You Pick Up Clutter Before the House Cleaner Arrives

Yes, picking up clutter before a cleaning visit helps the team work more efficiently and deliver better results. When surfaces are clear, cleaners spend their time scrubbing, dusting, and sanitizing rather than moving items around.

**Quick prep before the team arrives:**

- Put away clothes, shoes, and bags from floors
- Clear kitchen counters of dishes and food
- Pick up toys and pet items from living areas
- Secure pets in a safe area or let the team know in advance
- Leave clean sheets out if you want beds made
- Note any priority areas or specific instructions

## What's Not Worth Paying Someone to Clean for You

Some tasks are better handled yourself between professional visits rather than paying for them as part of a recurring clean. Knowing where to draw the line helps you get the most value from your cleaning budget.

**Handle these yourself:**

- Daily dishwashing and counter wipes
- Quick bathroom sink wipe-downs between visits
- Making beds daily
- Sweeping entryways during rainy weeks
- Pet feeding and litter box maintenance
- Sorting mail and daily clutter

**Worth paying for:**

- Thorough bathroom sanitizing (toilets, tubs, showers)
- Kitchen deep wipe-down including stovetop and microwave
- Full-home dusting and vacuuming
- Mopping all hard floors
- Baseboard and detail work (via deep cleaning)

The goal of recurring cleaning is to handle the tasks that take real time and effort, not the 5-minute daily upkeep you can manage yourself.

## FAQ

### What does house cleaning include in a standard visit?

A standard visit includes kitchen and bathroom sanitizing, dusting all accessible surfaces, vacuuming carpets and rugs, mopping hard floors, emptying trash, and general tidying of living areas and bedrooms.

### Does recurring cleaning include deep cleaning tasks?

No. Recurring cleaning maintains surfaces. Deep cleaning tasks like full baseboard scrubs, inside-oven cleaning, and detailed grout work require a separate deep cleaning appointment.

### Can I request specific rooms be skipped?

Yes. During your walkthrough, you can specify which rooms to clean and which to skip. The cleaning plan adjusts to your priorities.

### Do cleaners bring their own supplies?

Most professional cleaning teams bring their own supplies and equipment. If you prefer specific products, discuss this during the walkthrough.

### Is pet hair removal included?

Yes, vacuuming and visible surface pet hair removal is part of standard floor care. Deep pet stain or odor treatment may require a separate service.

### How do I prepare for a recurring cleaning visit?

Pick up personal items from floors and counters, secure pets, and leave out any priority instructions. You do not need to pre-clean surfaces.

### What happens if I am not home during the cleaning?

Many clients provide access through a key, garage code, or door lockbox. The team follows your cleaning plan and leaves a note or contacts you with any questions.
